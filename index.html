For my first project in IDEAS 120, I had to make a Flarf poem, which had us get the latest text message we sent, put it into google search, scrape the results, and then Frankenstein the words to make a poem out of them. My last text I sent was a joking one, saying, "Aidan when are we gonna do a Mario sluggers 999 challenge". As a result, my google search results were extremely goofy, so I decided to make my poem more comedy orientated. I enjoyed the results, and you can find the poem at this link: https://shplop.github.io/PROJECT_1_RMCMANUS/

PROJECT #2


For my project two, I was tasked with making a looping zoom background. My immediate thoughts went to a rollercoaster, as I was inspired by the youtube videos where people pretend that they are riding a roller coaster when it is just the background. I did not want to just steal someone's youtube video, so I decided to make a minecraft rollercoaster as a replacement. Unfortunately, I broke my laptop, which contained the footage of me using the background, but I still have footage of the rollercoaster I made.

PROJECT #3


Project 3 was easily my favorite of my works. We were tasked with making a Twine game, which is something I had done before. Inspired by recent books I read such as the Southern Reach Trilogy by Jeff VanderMeer and the Sun by Ethan Rutherford, I decided to make my game set in whaling times in the 1800s. My last game was horror, but for this one, I just wanted to make a sense of eerieness, something I hope came across in the work. I had to research a lot about the time period and sailing traditions in order to make it feel like said time period. The game has three endings, with only one of them being a defined "bad ending". I feel as if I improved greatly from my first game, and it was the work I was most proud about this semester. Link to the game: https://shplop.github.io/Voyage-of-the-Whaleship-Erasmus/
